The core difference between the **Simple Present** and **Present Continuous** lies in how we view the timeline of a business event. 

### 1. Simple Present (Loops & Schedules)
Use this tense for permanent facts, habitual actions, and scheduled timetables:
* **Habits/Routines**: Actions that repeat over time.
* **Timetables**: Future events controlled by an official schedule (meetings, flights, conferences).

### 2. Present Continuous (Snapshots & Trends)
Use this tense for events happening around the moment of speaking or temporary situations:
* **Ongoing Actions**: Current projects that are not yet completed.
* **Trends**: Gradual changes in the market or business environment.
* **Temporary situations**: Shifts that will end soon.
