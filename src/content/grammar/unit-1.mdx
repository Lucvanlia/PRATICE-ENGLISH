The core difference between Simple Present and Present Continuous lies in how we view the timeline of a business event.

### 1. Simple Present (Habits and Schedules)
Use for permanent facts, habitual actions, and scheduled timetables:
* **Habits**: Actions that repeat regularly over time.
* **Timetables**: Future events on a fixed schedule (meetings, flights, trains).
* **Facts**: Permanent states or truths.

### 2. Present Continuous (Snapshots and Trends)
Use for events happening around the moment of speaking or temporary situations:
* **Ongoing actions**: Current projects not yet completed.
* **Temporary situations**: Shifts that will end soon.
* **Trends**: Gradual changes in business environment.

### 3. Stative Verbs (Never Continuous)
These verbs describe states, not actions, and cannot be used in continuous form:
> know, believe, understand, like, love, hate, own, belong, mean, need, want, prefer, see, hear, smell, taste
