Relative clauses are useful for adding descriptive details to subjects or objects. They appear frequently in Part 5 gap-fillers and Part 7 passages.

### 1. Relative Pronouns as Subjects vs. Objects
* **Subject**: The pronoun replaces the subject in the sub-clause. It is followed by a verb: `Noun + who/which + Verb`.
* **Object**: The pronoun replaces the object. It is followed by a new subject: `Noun + whom/which + Subject + Verb`.

### 2. The Comma and Preposition Rules
* **Commas**: If the clause is extra information (non-defining), use commas, and *never* use *that*.
* **Prepositions**: You can move a preposition to the front of the relative pronoun. In this case, use *whom* (people) or *which* (things).
