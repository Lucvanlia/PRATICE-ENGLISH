### Defining vs Non-Defining Relative Clauses

| Type | Commas? | Pronoun | Effect |
|------|---------|---------|--------|
| **Defining** | No commas | who, which, that, whose | Specifies which one |
| **Non-defining** | Commas present | who, which, whose (NOT that) | Adds extra info |

**Defining**: The employee **who was promoted** deserves recognition. (tells us which employee)

**Non-defining**: The CEO, **who was promoted last year**, announced the restructure. (just extra info about the CEO)

### Omitting the Relative Pronoun
When the relative pronoun is the **object** of the clause (not the subject), it can be omitted:
> The report **(that/which)** she submitted was approved. → "The report she submitted was approved."

When it is the **subject**, it cannot be omitted:
> The manager **who** approved the report is on leave. ✓ (cannot remove 'who')
