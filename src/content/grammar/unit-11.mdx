Passive voice is highly prevalent in business writing, where the focus is on the action or the result rather than who performed it.

### 1. Structure of Passive Voice
Passive sentences are constructed using the auxiliary verb **be** (conjugated to the correct tense) and the **past participle (V3/ed)**:
* **The Receiver**: The object of the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence.
* **The Agent**: The performer of the action is introduced using *by*. It is often omitted if obvious or unknown.

### 2. Intransitive Verbs Exception
Intransitive verbs do not take direct objects. Therefore, they cannot be made passive. Watch out for these common TOEIC traps:
* *occur, happen, take place*
* *arrive, go, come*
* *rise, fall, remain, exist*
* *look, seem, appear*
