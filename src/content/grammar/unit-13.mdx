Conjunctions vs. Prepositions is one of the top 3 most frequently tested topics in Part 5 of the TOEIC exam. Knowing how to analyze the sentence structure after the blank gives you the answer in seconds.

### 1. Conjunctions (Connecting Clauses)
Conjunctions connect two complete clauses containing a subject and a verb:
* **Contrast**: *although, even though, though, while, whereas*.
* **Reason**: *because, since, as, now that*.
* **Condition**: *if, unless, provided that, assuming that*.

### 2. Prepositions (Connecting Noun Phrases)
Prepositions introduce noun phrases, pronouns, or gerunds:
* **Contrast**: *despite, in spite of*.
* **Reason**: *because of, due to, owing to, on account of*.
* **Time**: *during, in the event of*.
