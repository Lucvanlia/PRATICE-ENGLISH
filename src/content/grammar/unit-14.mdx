Conditional sentences represent different degrees of probability. Understanding their structures and inversion forms is highly helpful for solving Part 5 grammar questions.

### 1. The Three Core Types
* **Type 1 (Possible)**: Future possibility. Structure: `If + Present, Will + V-bare`.
* **Type 2 (Hypothetical)**: Contrary to present facts. Structure: `If + Past, Would + V-bare`.
* **Type 3 (Unreal Past)**: Contrary to past facts, expressing regret or past scenarios. Structure: `If + Past Perfect, Would have + V3/ed`.

### 2. Inversion of Conditionals
Dropping the word *if* and inversion changes the sentence structure:
* **Type 1 Inversion**: `Should + Subject + V-bare`.
* **Type 2 Inversion**: `Were + Subject + to-V` (or `Were + Subject + Noun/Adj`).
* **Type 3 Inversion**: `Had + Subject + V3/ed`.
