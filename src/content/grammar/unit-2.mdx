The core difference between **Present Perfect** and **Present Perfect Continuous** lies in whether you want to highlight the **product** (result) or the **process** (duration) of the action.

### 1. Present Perfect (Result & Achievement)
Use this tense when the focus is on the completion of the action, the total amount achieved, or the number of times it has occurred:
* **Quantities/Counts**: Emphasizes *how many* or *how much*.
* **State Change**: Represents that an action occurred at an unspecified time, affecting the present.

### 2. Present Perfect Continuous (Process & Duration)
Use this tense to emphasize that an action started in the past, has been happening continuously, and is still in progress:
* **Ongoing Work**: Emphasizes *how long* the action has been going on.
* **Temporary Loops**: Continuous effort that may or may not finish soon.
