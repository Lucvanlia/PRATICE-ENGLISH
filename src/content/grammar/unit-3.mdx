The relationship between the **Past Simple** and **Past Continuous** is often about foreground vs. background. 

### 1. Past Simple (The Anchor)
Use this tense to express actions that were completed in the past at a definite time:
* **Historical Facts**: Dates, years, and specific past events.
* **Sequence of Events**: Actions happening one after another in a narrative.
* **Completed States**: General situations in the past that are no longer true.

### 2. Past Continuous (The Scenery)
Use this tense to describe an action that was ongoing in the past:
* **Background Context**: Setting the scene for past reporting.
* **Interrupted Actions**: An ongoing past process (long) interrupted by another completed past action (short).
* **Parallel Actions**: Two or more actions happening simultaneously in the past (using *while*).
