The **Past Perfect** tenses are essential for indicating the chronological order of two related past events. They act as a marker for the "earlier past."

### 1. Past Perfect (The First Completed Action)
Use this tense to make it clear that one action occurred and completed before another action in the past:
* **Sequencing**: Clear representation of what happened first.
* **By the time**: Typically paired with Past Simple to represent a deadline in the past.

### 2. Past Perfect Continuous (The First Ongoing Action)
Use this tense to emphasize the duration or ongoing process of the earlier action:
* **Duration before past point**: Shows *how long* something was happening up until another past milestone.
* **Cause of past result**: Explains the background cause of a past condition.
