Expressing future events in TOEIC depends heavily on the source of the decision: whether it is a spontaneous reaction or a pre-conceived plan.

### 1. Simple Future (Will)
Use this tense for actions decided at the moment of speaking or general predictions:
* **Spontaneous Decisions**: Quick reactions or answers to current situations.
* **Offers & Promises**: Formal business commitments.
* **Predictions without evidence**: Gut feelings or general thoughts about the future.

### 2. Near Future (Be Going To)
Use this tense when the future event is already planned or is imminent due to present circumstances:
* **Prior Intention**: Projects and arrangements decided before speaking.
* **Predictions with evidence**: Foretelling an event based on visible indicators.
