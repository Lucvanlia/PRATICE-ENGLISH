### Modal Verb Functions at a Glance

| Modal | Function | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **can** | Ability, permission (informal) | He can manage the project. |
| **could** | Past ability, polite request | Could you send the file? |
| **must** | Strong obligation, confident deduction | You must register by Friday. |
| **should** | Advice, recommendation | You should back up your files. |
| **may** | Formal permission, possibility | You may enter. / It may be delayed. |
| **might** | Weaker possibility | The shipment might arrive late. |
| **will** | Future intention, certainty | I will confirm by tomorrow. |

### Past Modals (Modal + have + V3)
Used to express past possibility, regret, or deduction:
* **Should have + V3**: You should have double-checked. (regret/criticism)
* **Must have + V3**: She must have left early. (confident past deduction)
* **Could have + V3**: They could have emailed us. (past possibility not taken)
