title: "Articles & Nouns – Mạo Từ & Danh Từ"
module: 2
objective: "Master the use of 'a', 'an', 'the', and zero article with countable, uncountable, and proper nouns to avoid the most common TOEIC Part 5 traps."
formula: "a/an (indefinite) + the (definite) + Ø (zero article)"
formulas:
  - label: "A / AN – Indefinite Article (Mạo từ không xác định)"
    structure: "a + consonant sound | an + vowel sound"
    example: "a meeting, an agenda, an hour (silent h)"
  - label: "THE – Definite Article (Mạo từ xác định)"
    structure: "the + noun (specific or unique)"
    example: "the conference room, the CEO, the results"
  - label: "Zero Article (Không mạo từ)"
    structure: "Ø + plural noun | Ø + uncountable noun"
    example: "Information is important. / Meetings were cancelled."
examples:
  - eng: "We need to hire a project manager for the new initiative."
    vie: "Chúng ta cần tuyển một quản lý dự án cho sáng kiến mới này."
    note: "'a project manager' = unspecified one person. 'the new initiative' = specific, already known."
  - eng: "The equipment delivered yesterday is defective. Please check the warranty."
    vie: "Thiết bị được giao hôm qua bị lỗi. Hãy kiểm tra bảo hành."
    note: "'The equipment' = specific delivery. 'the warranty' = specific document."
commonMistakes:
  - mistake: "She gave me a advice about the project."
    correction: "She gave me advice about the project."
    explanation: "'Advice' is an uncountable noun. No article (use zero article). Say 'a piece of advice' if you need a count."
  - mistake: "We need the manager to approve this before we proceed."
    correction: "Correct IF both speaker and listener know which manager. If not: 'We need a manager to approve this.'"
    explanation: "Use 'the' only when both parties know what is being referred to (second mention or unique reference)."
memoryTips:
  - "A / AN = first mention or non-specific: 'I need a pen.'"
  - "THE = specific or already mentioned: 'Here is the pen you asked for.'"
  - "Zero article = uncountable (information, advice, equipment) or general plural (employees work hard)."
cheatSheet:
  - "an + vowel SOUND: an hour, an MBA, an umbrella (not 'a hour')"
  - "Uncountable nouns (no article): advice, information, equipment, furniture, news, luggage"
  - "Unique things use 'the': the sun, the internet, the president of the company"
  - "Second mention → the: 'I received a report. The report was incomplete.'"
summary: "Use 'a/an' for first mention or non-specific. Use 'the' for specific or unique. Use zero article for uncountable and plural general nouns."
flashcards:
  - front: "Article for 'advice'?"
    back: "Zero article (uncountable) — just 'advice', not 'an advice'"
  - front: "What article rule applies to second mention?"
    back: "Use 'the' — first mention = a/an, second = the"
  - front: "'A' or 'an' before 'MBA'?"
    back: "an MBA ('M' sounds like 'em' = vowel sound)"
  - front: "When do you use zero article with countable nouns?"
    back: "General plural statements: 'Employees are required to sign in.'"
miniQuiz:
  - question: "She submitted ____ report and ____ director approved it immediately."
    options:
      - "a / the"
      - "the / a"
      - "a / a"
      - "the / the"
    answer: "a / the"
    explanation: "'A report' = first mention, non-specific. 'The director' = already established/known person."
  - question: "Which is CORRECT?"
    options:
      - "He gave me an useful advice."
      - "He gave me a useful advice."
      - "He gave me useful advice."
      - "He gave me the useful advice."
    answer: "He gave me useful advice."
    explanation: "'Advice' is uncountable → zero article. 'An advice' and 'a advice' are always wrong."
homework:
  - question: "Fill in: We are looking for _____ experienced accountant to join _____ finance department."
    answer: "an experienced accountant (vowel sound, first mention) / the finance department (specific department in the company)"
  - question: "Why is 'The unemployment is rising' incorrect?"
    answer: "Uncountable abstract nouns used in general statements take zero article: 'Unemployment is rising.'"

### Article Decision Tree

```
Is the noun countable?
├── YES
│   ├── First mention / non-specific → a / an
│   └── Second mention / specific / unique → the
└── NO (uncountable)
    ├── General statement → Ø (zero article)
    └── Specific reference → the (e.g. the information you sent)
```

### Common Uncountable Nouns in Business English
> advice, information, equipment, furniture, luggage, news, traffic, accommodation, feedback, knowledge, progress, research, work, travel
