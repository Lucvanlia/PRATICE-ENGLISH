Comparison forms are widely tested in the TOEIC reading section, especially when analyzing charts, tables, or comparing products and employee performance.

### 1. Equality (as ... as)
Compares identical or equivalent items:
* **Active Form**: `as + Adjective/Adverb + as`.
* **Modifiers**: `nearly, almost, just, twice, three times` are placed before the first *as*.

### 2. Comparative & Superlative Rules
* **Comparative**: Compares two entities, signaled by the word *than*.
* **Superlative**: Compares one entity against a group, signaled by *the* and group markers like *in the world, of all, year-to-date*.
