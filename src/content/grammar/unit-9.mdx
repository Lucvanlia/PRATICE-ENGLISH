### Gerund vs Infinitive: The Three Categories

**Category 1 – Gerund ONLY (V-ing)**
> enjoy, avoid, consider, suggest, recommend, risk, postpone, miss, admit, deny, finish, keep, practise

**Category 2 – Infinitive ONLY (to + V1)**
> agree, decide, expect, hope, manage, offer, plan, promise, refuse, want, wish, arrange, fail

**Category 3 – Both, but meaning changes**

| Verb | + Gerund (V-ing) | + Infinitive (to V1) |
|------|-----------------|---------------------|
| remember | remember doing (past action) | remember to do (future reminder) |
| forget | forget doing (past action) | forget to do (neglect) |
| try | try doing (experiment) | try to do (attempt/effort) |
| stop | stop doing (quit) | stop to do (pause) |
| regret | regret doing (past action) | regret to say (formal announcement) |

### After Prepositions: Always Gerund
Any preposition (in, on, at, for, about, with, without, before, after, instead of, etc.) is always followed by **V-ing**:
> She is interested **in** *learning* new software.
> He apologised **for** *missing* the deadline.
