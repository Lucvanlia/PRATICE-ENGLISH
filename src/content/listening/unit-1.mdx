TOEIC Part 1 photo descriptions that contain only objects and no people require a specific listening mindset. Since there are no humans to perform actions, the correct statements must describe the **static status** or **spatial arrangements** of the items.

### 1. The Power of Prepositions
In object-only photos, the location of the objects relative to each other is highly critical. Pay close attention to prepositions such as:
* `against`: leaning against a wall or pillar.
* `along`: parked along the curb, aligned along the edge.
* `on top of`: stacked on top of each other.
* `under / below`: placed under the desk.

### 2. Spotting the 'Being' Trap
The most common distractor in object-only photos is the use of the present continuous passive voice:
**Subject + am/is/are + BEING + Past Participle (V3/ed)**
* **Rule**: If there is no human agent in the picture, a choice using this structure is incorrect because it implies an action currently in progress (e.g. "the grass is being cut", "the car is being repaired").
* **Exceptions**: Sometimes "being" is correct if it refers to passive states not requiring active visible human effort, such as:
  * "Shadows are being cast on the ground."
  * "Goods are being displayed for sale."
