Part 4 is the final section of TOEIC Listening and consists of 10 monologues from a single speaker. Unlike Part 3, there is no dialogue — only one voice delivering information.

### 1. The 9 Common Talk Formats
Each format has characteristic opening phrases:
* **Phone message**: "You've reached the voicemail of..." / "Thank you for calling..."
* **Announcement**: "Attention all passengers..." / "Ladies and gentlemen..."
* **Advertisement**: "Looking for great deals?" / "Don't miss out!"
* **News report**: "In business news today..." / "Reporting live from..."
* **Tour guide**: "Welcome to our facility..." / "On your left, you'll see..."

### 2. The Three-Question Pattern
Each talk typically contains:
1. **Main topic question**: What is the talk mainly about? → Listen to the first 10 seconds.
2. **Detail question**: What does the speaker say about X? → Target specific audio evidence.
3. **Action/Audience question**: What are listeners asked to do? / Who is the intended audience?
