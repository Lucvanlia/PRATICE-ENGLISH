Yes/No and Choice questions form a substantial portion of Part 2. Yes/No questions require agreement/disagreement patterns, while Choice questions need a selection or indirect bypass.

### 1. Yes/No Questions
The key is that responses do NOT always literally start with "Yes" or "No." Indirect responses are common:
* **Positive indirect**: "Of course," "Absolutely," "I believe so."
* **Negative indirect**: "I'm afraid not," "Actually, it was cancelled," "Not yet."

### 2. Choice Questions (A or B?)
Choice questions present two options. Valid responses include:
* **Single selection**: "I'd prefer A."
* **Accepting both**: "Either works for me."
* **Rejecting both**: "Neither is available at that time."
* **Indirect**: "It depends on the budget."
