Part 3's two most difficult question types are implication questions and graphic-matching questions. Both require deeper processing than simple detail recall.

### 1. Implication Questions
These questions test whether you understand the speaker's true intent:
* **Sarcasm**: The literal meaning is the opposite of what is meant.
* **Hedged Language**: "It's not exactly what we expected" = disappointment.
* **Ellipsis**: What is *not* said is as important as what is said.

### 2. Graphic Questions
One graphic (table, map, schedule, or list) is provided per conversation set:
* **Before listening**: Identify the graphic type and scan the key categories.
* **During listening**: Find ONE statement that matches ONE row of the graphic.
* **Eliminate**: The audio will provide one clue. Three rows will be eliminated by context.
